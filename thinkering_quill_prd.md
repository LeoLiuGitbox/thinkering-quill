## 1. Product Overview

**Product Name:** Thinkering Quill
**Category:** Story-driven adaptive learning platform for children
**Primary Platform:** Local web app (browser-based, runs on home computer)
**MVP Target:** Year 5 children (age 10–11) preparing for WA GATE selective entry exams

**Core Fantasy Theme:**
A magical academy where children grow from **Novice Scribe** to **Master of the Quill** by using real thinking, logic, and problem-solving. Every skill learned in the Archive Hall is the same magic that powers the real world.

**Positioning Statement:**
Thinkering Quill is a story-driven learning adventure that turns reasoning, persistence, and curiosity into magical growth. Children solve meaningful challenges, earn magical artifacts, discover how their skills live in the real world, and shape their identity as a wise mage — while a gentle integrity system reflects the difference between earned knowledge and empty shortcuts.

**Current Product Focus Clarification:**
Thinkering Quill is currently a **highly targeted WA GATE / ASET preparation app**. The three objective sections — **Quantitative Reasoning, Abstract Reasoning, and Reading Comprehension** — remain strong exam-focused training loops built around repetition, strengthening, progression, and feedback. The **Writing** module is intentionally different: it is being redesigned as a **writing coaching system**, not a one-shot "write a full piece and let AI score it" experience. Its purpose is to build real writing ability through skill drills, scaffolds, revision, and staged composition practice.

**Core Philosophy (产品灵魂):**
> 考试是为了学习知识，知识是为了解决实际问题。把现实活成游戏。
> 熟悉 + 惊喜 = 幽默。兴趣是最好的老师。

Tests exist to build knowledge. Knowledge exists to solve real problems.
The magic of the Archive Hall is the same magic that runs the real world.
Familiar thinking + an unexpected scenario = delight. Interest is the best teacher.

## 2. Product Vision

The app must make children feel:

1. **Thinking is magical** — not homework with decorations
2. **Effort changes the world** — every quest visibly advances the story
3. **Honest learning is more powerful than shortcuts** — the Shadow Drift system mirrors this
4. **Mistakes are part of mastery** — failure is a learning event, never a punishment
5. **Knowledge lives in the real world** — every skill practised in the Archive Hall is already alive outside it

The app should not feel like homework. It should feel like a living magical world powered by curiosity, reasoning, and integrity — where the greatest discovery is that real life runs on the same spells.

## 4. Core Product Pillars

### 4.1 Thinking First
Every progression mechanic must be tied to actual reasoning. Passive tapping earns nothing.

### 4.2 Story-Driven Learning
Challenges are embedded in a magical journey. The curriculum is invisible; the adventure is real.

### 4.3 Knowledge Connects to Life
After every session, the child discovers how the skills they just used are alive in the real world. This is not an add-on — it is the soul of the product.

### 4.4 Integrity Matters
The product rewards authentic learning and reflects shortcut behaviour through narrative — never shame.

### 4.6 Adaptive Confidence
80% of challenges come from known territory (familiar → confidence). 20% push into new ground (challenge → growth). Yesterday's 20% becomes tomorrow's 80%.

## 5. Core Narrative

In the world of **Thinkering Quill**, every child receives a mysterious feather quill from the Great Archive of Magic. This quill responds not to power, but to **thought**.

The world is powered by five ancient schools of magic:

| School | Domain | Player Attribute |
|---|---|---|
| **Logic** | Order, deduction, sequences, proof | Logic |
| **Insight** | Pattern recognition, abstraction, analogies | Insight |
| **Craft** | Step-by-step construction, creative expression | Craft |
| **Focus** | Memory, attention, persistence, comprehension | Focus |
| **Wisdom** | Reflection, ethics, choosing the right path | Wisdom |

The child begins as a **Novice Scribe** in a magical academy. To grow, they must restore forgotten realms, solve rune puzzles, unlock ancient tools, and help magical creatures and kingdoms — using the same thinking that doctors, chefs, athletes, and architects use every day.

The Quill can sense whether knowledge is truly earned. Rapid guessing, hint abuse, and shortcuts corrupt the Quill's light. The child's aura darkens — not as punishment, but as a narrative mirror: *power taken without understanding casts only shadows*.

Recovery is always possible through honest effort and reflection.

## 9. 80/20 Adaptive Session Composition

### The Principle
> 熟悉建立自信，惊喜推动成长。

Every practice session is composed of:
- **80% familiar** — topics where mastery level ≥ 3 (confident territory)
- **20% challenge** — topics where mastery ≤ 2, or topics never attempted

The "familiar" questions are not boring repeats — Claude is instructed to wrap familiar reasoning in **fresh, surprising, or humorous scenarios**. Same spell, new costume.

### Mastery Scoring (per knowledge point)

Tracked in `KnowledgeMastery` table after every session:

```
masteryScore = (
  firstChoiceAccuracy × 0.50 +   ← most honest signal of true understanding
  finalAccuracy       × 0.25 +   ← adjusted for hint use
  speedScore          × 0.25     ← time vs. expected pace
) × hintPenaltyFactor

hintPenaltyFactor = 1.0 - (avgHintsUsed × 0.15)
speedScore = clamp(expectedTimeMs / actualTimeMs, 0.0, 1.0)
```

| Score | Level | Label |
|---|---|---|
| 0.0 – 0.3 | 1 | 🌱 Seedling |
| 0.3 – 0.55 | 2 | 🌿 Growing |
| 0.55 – 0.75 | 3 | 🌳 Developing |
| 0.75 – 0.9 | 4 | ⭐ Strong |
| 0.9 – 1.0 | 5 | 💎 Mastered |

### Per-Question Analytics

Every MCQ attempt records:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `firstChoiceCorrect` | Was the first selection correct? (before any change) — truest mastery signal |
| `finalAnswerCorrect` | Was the submitted answer correct? |
| `timeSpentMs` | Total time from display to submit |
| `minimumReadTimeMs` | Enforced minimum read time (prevents rapid tapping) |
| `hintsUsed` | Number of hint scrolls consulted |
| `changedAnswer` | Did the student change their first selection? |
| `knowledgePointCode` | Which topic this question tests |

## 10. The 熟悉 + 惊喜 = 幽默 Principle in Question Generation

When Claude generates a batch of questions, the prompt explicitly instructs:

> "For 1–2 of the familiar-topic questions, wrap the same reasoning in an unexpected, funny, or magical scenario. A probability question about dragons stealing gems from a wizard's vault tests the same skill as marbles in a jar — but surprises and delights instead of boring. Keep the math identical; change the costume entirely."

This creates the moment children love: *I already know how to do this — but wait, this is hilarious.* That moment of recognition and delight is the engine of intrinsic motivation.

## 11. "Spells in the Wild" — Real World Connection

### The Core Idea
> 考试是为了学习知识，知识是为了解决实际问题。

After every practice session or tournament, before the reward screen:

> *"Your quill glows. It has sensed this magic in the world beyond the Archive…"*

The app shows **1–2 short stories** (150–200 words each) connecting the knowledge points just practised to a vivid, real-life scenario. The child discovers that the exact thinking they used in the wizard challenge is alive in the real world right now.

### Story Rules
1. **Real protagonist** — a specific person with a specific job
2. **Concrete numbers** — the story actually performs the reasoning with real quantities
3. **"Spot the spell"** — ends with: *"Can you see where [spell name] appeared?"*
4. **60–90 seconds to read** — short, specific, punchy
5. **Any tone** — funny, dramatic, mysterious, surprising
6. **Age-appropriate** — food, sport, nature, animals, technology, adventure

### Real World Story Examples

| Knowledge Point | Story Seed |
|---|---|
| QR-02 Probability | A weather forecaster tells a farmer: "3 in 5 chance of rain tomorrow — save the watering?" |
| QR-03 Combinatorics | A chef designing a set menu calculates how many 3-course combinations are possible |
| QR-06 Time & Rate | A paramedic calculates how long a 500ml IV bag will last at 120ml/hour |
| QR-13 Logic puzzles | A detective with three suspects and three contradicting stories — only one can be true |
| AR-01 Rotation | An architect rotating a floor plan to check how afternoon sunlight enters each room |
| AR-07 Combination rules | A game programmer: "When two power-ups overlap, the player gets both effects combined" |
| RC-03 Inference | A doctor reading between the lines of a patient's vague symptoms to reach a diagnosis |
| RC-06 Charts | A nurse spotting an unusual trend in a vital signs chart before symptoms appear |

### The Field Journal (野外日志)

Every "Spells in the Wild" story is saved to the student's personal **Field Journal** — a parchment-style notebook accessible from the nav bar.

- Organised by knowledge point code
- Each entry: story + "spot the spell" question + the student's own answer (optional, earns +5✦)
- Collecting all stories for one knowledge point unlocks a **"Living Spell"** badge
- Parents can read the journal — it is the most meaningful artefact of learning in the app
- Over time, the journal becomes a living map of how the child connects thinking to life

### Session Flow with "Spells in the Wild"

```
Complete session
      ↓
"Spells in the Wild" screen
  (Claude generates 1–2 stories based on top knowledge points from session)
      ↓
Reflection prompt  ("How did you figure out the trickiest question?")
      ↓
Reward screen  (Sparks ✦ + artifact + badge check)
      ↓
Back to Archive Hall
```

## 13. Pre-Generation + Navigation Model

All practice sessions pre-generate every question before the student begins. No waiting mid-session.

### Session Start
1. Student selects: **Zone** + **Question count** (5 / 10 / 15 / 35) + **Difficulty** (Apprentice / Journeyman / Archmage)
2. Loading screen: *"Preparing your challenges…"* — Claude generates all questions in one API call using the 80/20 topic mix
3. First question displayed immediately

### In-Session Navigation
```
[← Previous]   Question 3 of 10   [Skip ⚑]   [Next →]

 ✦ ✦ ● ○ ⚑ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○    ← crystal navigator
   answered  current  skipped  unanswered
```

- **Previous / Next**: freely move between questions
- **Skip**: flags with ⚑, moves to next — can return any time
- **Jump**: tap any crystal in the navigator
- **Submit**: available after all questions viewed; confirms unanswered count

### Hint System — Ancient Scroll (costs 🪶 Quill Energy)

| Scroll | Energy Cost | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Scroll 1 | −2 🪶 | Socratic guiding question — nudges without giving away the answer |
| Scroll 2 | −3 🪶 | Eliminates one wrong option + names the reasoning strategy |

- Hints cached client-side by `questionHash` — same question never re-costs
- Practice mode: energy cost only. Grand Tournament: energy + −3✦ Sparks per scroll
- **AR hints**: include a visual annotation overlay highlighting relevant SVG grid cells
- **CIIW**: no hints (matches real ASET); "Inspiration Nudge" (−1🪶) offers one spark of direction without giving content

## 14. Subject Detail: Communicating Ideas in Writing (CIIW)

### Official ASET Format
- 25 minutes, one written response
- Prompt: image, text, or both
- No genre/style restriction
- Marked by minimum 2 independent markers

### Official Marking Criteria (7 dimensions)
1. Relevance to the prompt *(must be central — not superficial)*
2. Complexity, freshness, and interest of ideas
3. Style and form, and how they enhance ideas
4. Strength of plot / message / issue / information
5. Organisation and coherence
6. Distinctiveness of voice and tone
7. Appropriateness, expressiveness, and fluency of language

### Writing Module Redesign Principles
Writing in Thinkering Quill is **not** primarily a simulation of an automatic scoring teacher. It is a **coaching system** designed to improve real writing ability.

Core principles:
- Writing training prioritises:
  - topic selection
  - idea generation and clarity of intent
  - structure and organisation
  - detail expansion
  - `show, not tell`
  - revision and improvement
- AI is used mainly to:
  - generate scaffolds
  - provide examples and contrasts
  - give specific revision prompts
  - coach the student through rewriting
- AI is **not** the long-term primary judge of writing quality, and should not be treated as equivalent to official human marking
- Full ASET-style writing tasks remain important, but they are **stage checks**, not the default daily training unit

### Writing Skill Areas
The Writing module is organised around trainable sub-skills rather than only full-piece submission.

- **Idea generation** — choosing a workable angle and central idea
- **Prompt interpretation** — understanding what the prompt invites without writing off-topic
- **Structure and organisation** — openings, paragraphing, progression, endings
- **Expression and style** — vivid phrasing, sentence variation, tone
- **Show, not tell** — using actions, sensory detail, and scene evidence instead of flat explanation
- **Revision and improvement** — identifying weaknesses and rewriting with purpose
- **Language control** — fluency, precision, grammar, and sentence clarity

Each skill area can be:
- trained independently
- given targeted feedback
- tracked as gradual progress over time

### Training Model — Writing as Coaching, Not Just Scoring
The default writing experience is redesigned into three layers:

#### `micro skill drill`
- 5–8 minutes
- trains one writing skill at a time
- examples:
  - convert `tell` into `show`
  - improve an opening hook
  - rewrite a flat sentence with sensory detail
  - expand a paragraph with stronger evidence or imagery
  - choose the strongest central idea from several options

#### `guided writing`
- 10–15 minutes
- semi-structured writing focused on one part of a full piece
- examples:
  - write only the opening
  - write only the middle conflict / turning point
  - create a quick outline
  - improve a weak ending
  - reorganise a paragraph sequence for clarity

#### `full writing task`
- used periodically
- preserves the ASET-style experience
- can still use image, text, or mixed prompts
- used to check whether the student can transfer trained skills into a full response
- not used as the default everyday training format

### New Writing Feedback Contract
Default feedback should be short, specific, and revision-oriented.

Each writing interaction should aim to produce:
- `one strength`
- `one priority issue`
- `one revision instruction`
- `optional model example`
- `optional retry / rewrite step`

Feedback principles:
- prioritise "what to improve next" over broad summary judgment
- do not default to showing all 7 ASET scoring dimensions after every task
- do not let praise become generic or empty
- keep revision tasks small enough that the student can immediately act on them

### Writing Training Loop
The core loop for daily writing practice is:

1. Choose a writing skill
2. View one strong and one weak example
3. Complete a short writing task
4. Receive one specific revision prompt
5. Rewrite immediately
6. Compare before and after
7. Update skill progress
8. Return to a full writing task only after repeated skill practice

This replaces the weaker loop of:

`write once -> receive a score -> stop`

### Image Prompt Role (Retained but Reduced)
Image prompts remain part of the product because they match ASET-style stimulus variety, but their role is reduced.

- image prompts are primarily used in `full writing task`
- `micro skill drill` and `guided writing` do **not** require image generation by default
- the Writing module should not depend on image generation to begin training
- image + text prompts remain useful for staged assessment, not for every daily lesson

### Data and Tracking Requirements
Writing data must support progress over drafts and revisions, not just store a single final response.

Minimum data to track:
- session type
- target skill
- prompt
- outline or planning notes
- draft v1
- revision instruction
- draft v2
- feedback summary
- skill progress update

The system should avoid reducing writing history to only:
- one final composition
- one feedback JSON blob

### ASET Alignment
The official 7 ASET dimensions still matter, but they are used differently:
- they inform the long-term design of skill areas
- they guide full-task review and checkpoint tasks
- they do **not** dominate every daily interaction

This keeps the module aligned with the real exam while avoiding the low-yield pattern of "full essay every time, then AI score."

### CIIW Reward Direction
Writing rewards should celebrate effort, revision, and completion of meaningful training loops — not only final polished output.

This means rewards should be available for:
- completing a skill drill
- performing a revision step
- finishing a guided writing session
- completing a full task

Full-task writing can still receive larger celebration moments, but daily writing growth should come from coached improvement, not surprise-box scoring alone.

## 15. Shadow Drift System (Integrity Mechanic)

### Design Principle
This is **narrative consequence**, not moral judgment. The system never says "you cheated." It says: *"The Quill senses unstable magic."*

### Behaviour Signals Tracked

| Signal | Severity Weight |
|---|---|
| Answer time < minimum read time for question length | +15 |
| 3+ rapid retries on same question (< 5s each) | +20 |
| Hint used, then same wrong answer repeated | +10 |
| 2+ hints on 3+ consecutive questions | +12 |
| Sudden perfect score after persistent low accuracy | +8 |
| Reflection prompt skipped 3+ times in a row | +5 |

### Shadow Score → Aura States

| Score | Aura | Child Sees |
|---|---|---|
| 0–29 | ✨ Bright | Nothing — normal experience |
| 30–59 | ⚡ Unstable | After session: *"The Quill senses some instability…"* |
| 60–79 | 🌑 Shadow Creeping | Aura darkens; Shadow Vault region unlocks |
| 80+ | 🌑 Shadow Drift | XP halved until recovery quest completed |

### Recovery
- Complete a Restoration Quest in the Shadow Vault
- Consistent honest play for 3 sessions reduces score by 20/session
- Recovery earns Light Essence 🌟 and restores aura
- All learning content always accessible, regardless of aura state

### Safeguards (non-negotiable)
- Never act on a single signal — patterns only, with thresholds
- Never block content access
- Never use frightening imagery
- Child never sees a raw score — only aura symbol + narrative copy
- Parent dashboard shows plain-language explanation only: *"Some quick-guessing patterns were detected. Encourage your child to read each question before answering."*

## 17. Grand Tournament — Tower of Ascension

Mirrors the real ASET experience:

- Choose which tests to include (default: all 4, in official order)
- Full mode (official counts/times) or Abbreviated mode (half questions, half time)
- All questions generated in one batch before any timer starts
- Per-section countdown: gold → amber at 5 min → crimson at 2 min
- Crystal navigator: filled = answered, empty = unanswered, flagged = skipped
- No feedback shown during exam
- Auto-submit on timeout; 10-min break between CIIW and QR
- Post-tournament: score reveal → full review with explanations → "Train this zone" links
- "Spells in the Wild" runs after tournament too, across all subjects tested

## 25. Safety, Ethics & Child Psychology

- Shadow is fantasy symbolism — never moral condemnation
- Redemption always available, never time-locked
- No public leaderboards, no cross-child comparison
- No frightening imagery (Shadow Vault is mysterious, not horror)
- Parent dashboard shows only: plain-language summaries, no raw scores
- No content access blocked due to aura state
- Integrity signals require patterns, never single events

---

## 26. Non-Functional Requirements

- Runs fully locally (macOS, browser-based, 2017 iMac or better)
- Internet required only for AI API calls (Claude + DALL-E)
- Responsive design: desktop + tablet primary; mobile secondary
- No external accounts required (no sign-up, no cloud sync in MVP)
- Offline caching for generated questions within a session
- DALL-E images saved locally immediately (API URLs expire)
- All AI calls include graceful fallback if API unavailable
